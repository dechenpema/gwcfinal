    How much do you really know about education accessibility?

    Question 1

    15% of high-income students from the 2004 graduating class of high school enrolled in a highly selective college or university. What percent of low-income students from the 2004 graduating class enrolled in a highly selective college?

    10%
    2%
    5%
    13%

    The answer you put is wrong.

    Fact: 15% of high-income students from the 2004 graduating class of high school enrolled in a highly selective college or university compared to only 5% of middle-income graduates and 2% of low-income graduates.

    The answer you put is right.

    Fact: 15% of high-income students from the 2004 graduating class of high school enrolled in a highly selective college or university compared to only 5% of middle-income graduates and 2% of low-income graduates.

    Question 2

    In the 1980s, the gap between the reading and math skills of the wealthiest 10 percent of kids and poorest 10 percent was about 90 points on an 800-point SAT-type scale. What is the gap today?

    100 points
    70 points
    50 points
    125 points

    The answer you put is wrong.

    Fact: In the 1980s, the gap between the reading and math skills of the wealthiest 10 percent of kids and poorest 10 percent was about 90 points on an 800-point SAT-type scale. Three decades later, the gap has grown to 125 points.

    The answer you put is right.

    Fact: In the 1980s, the gap between the reading and math skills of the wealthiest 10 percent of kids and poorest 10 percent was about 90 points on an 800-point SAT-type scale. Three decades later, the gap has grown to 125 points.
