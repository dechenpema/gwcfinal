    Scholarship Donations

    The Dream U.S

    The DREAM.US is the nation’s largest college access and success program for DREAMers. Providing scholarships to help them pay for their college education.DREAMers are undocumented immigrant youth who came to this country at a young age.There are now 1.8 million DREAMers living in the U.S. 65,000 graduate from high school every year–wanting more than anything to get a college education and help their families and communities prosper.

    Gates Millennium Scholars

    Funded by the Bill and Melinda Gates foundation, Gates Millennium Scholarships reduce financial hardship for low-income minority families preparing to send members to college. African Americans, Hispanics, Native Americans/Alaskan Natives, and Asian Pacific Islanders are invited to apply for one of the 1000 annual Millennium Scholarships, each of which provides a full financial ride through graduation.Scholarships Focusing on Low Income Students and Families

    Jack Kent Cooke Scholarships

    Jack Kent Cooke Foundation distributes a series of educational scholarships for secondary school students, undergraduates and graduate students. The scholarships reward students from low-income families who exhibit hard work and a strong will to succeed. Financial need is a prerequisite for all of the Foundation’s awards, along with solid academic achievement and extracurricular participation.JKC scholarships addresses groups of students,from middle school-post-graduate studies.

    Organizations Supporting The Minorities

    United We Dream

    "When you’re undocumented, you face a lot of discrimination, and that creates a lot of fear. At United We Dream, we transform that fear into finding your voice. We empower people to develop their leadership, their organizing skills, and to develop our own campaigns to fight for justice and dignity for immigrants and all people. This is achieved through immigrant youth-led campaigns at the local, state, and federal level."

    Childhood Education International

    "Education is, and always will be, vitally important in building a strong foundation for life; therefore, every child deserves an opportunity to learn. The global challenge for education is about providing access to education and about ensuring education’s progress by transforming and reinventing education to meet the child’s needs,the needs of their community and workforce, and the emerging needs of a more dynamic and interconnected world.

    Questbridge

    Annually, about 30,000 talented low-income students nationally are academically qualified to attend the nation’s best colleges, but the majority of don’t apply. QuestBridge recruits, develops & supports motivated low-income students from high school-college to their early career – to be successful at America's best colleges, graduate schools, and companies.There need be no academic gap if we can bring our best low-income students into the system.
