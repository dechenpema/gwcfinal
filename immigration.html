    Language Barriers and Awareness

    Immigration and Language Barriers

    Migrant students face various disadvantages that affect their academic performance and their well-being. Fluency in the language spoken in the host country is one of these source factors. Language barriers intensify the lack of parental support and studying in a disadvantaged school or attending a school with a poor disciplinary climate.The greater the linguistic distance between the language spoken at home and the language of instruction, the less likely a student will attain baseline academic proficiency and report a sense of belonging at school.Language fluency among school-aged children ensures that they are able to make the most of the learning opportunities offered by schools. It also enables children with an immigrant background to participate actively in the social life of their school, and develop a sense of belonging at their school community and beyond. Children with an immigrant background with language difficulties are more likely to be bullied, discriminated against and are more vulnerable to emotional problems, such as depression and low self-esteem.Language barriers can also make it difficult for immigrant parents to help their children integrate into their new community. For instance, parents who are not fluent in the host-country language might be particularly at risk of being out of the labour force or experience economic hardship. This may obstruct them from providing the material resources or assistance, such as supporting them in school and course selection and encouraging quality study strategies, that could help in their childrenâ€™s education.
