    About Us

    As New Yorkers, we recognize how fortunate we are to have amazing educational opportunities and participate in programs like Girls Who Code. However, we know that others within our city and across the country might not understand the resources available to them, while the government does not provide adequate educational support to the most disadvantaged communities. Disparities in funding, support, and therefore educational quality hinder many Americans from achieving their full potential.

    Ana Melián

    Co-founder

    I am a rising junior at Horace Mann School in New York City. In my free time I enjoy playing volleyball, reading, listening to music, and playing the cello. I love travelling and exploring. My interest in education comes from my experience volunteering as an after school teacher for elementary school students around my high school’s community of the Bronx. Through this experience, I learned the importance of ensuring equal educational support for underserved communities, both here at home and around the world.
